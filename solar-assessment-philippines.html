How it works

From your bill to your number in three steps

The assessment is deliberately short. You do not need engineering drawings or an installer visit to get a realistic first answer.

  1. Enter your monthly bill and city. That anchors the estimate to the payment you actually want to replace — not a generic national average.
  2. See your potential. We combine your bill with location-specific sun-hours and rooftop data to size a system and estimate first-year production.
  3. Get your numbers. Recommended size, an honest installed-cost range at roughly ₱50,000 per kWp, estimated monthly savings and a payback window.
Why bill-backed

Averages are encouraging. Your bill is the real answer.

A ₱5,000 bill and a ₱20,000 bill lead to very different systems. Sizing from your actual consumption avoids the two classic mistakes: an oversized array that never pays back, and an undersized one that barely dents the bill. Every assumption in your report is shown, so you can question it before anyone tries to sell you anything.

Questions

Solar assessment questions

Is the solar assessment really free?

Yes, and it is free forever for homeowners. You can run it, try different system sizes and see estimated savings without paying or entering a card. Apolaki earns from installer partners, not from you.

How accurate is the estimate?

It is a solid, bill-anchored first estimate built from NASA POWER irradiance and Google Solar rooftop data, using a transparent ₱50,000/kWp cost basis. Final figures are confirmed by an installer during a site visit; our own methodology and validation notes are published on the About page.

Do I have to talk to an installer?

No. You see your full report first. You only connect with an installer if and when you choose to — no cold calls, and your details stay private until you do.

What do I need to start?

Just your typical monthly electricity bill and your city. That is enough for a realistic first estimate; you can refine the roof size and address for a sharper number.
