Local context

Electricity and solar in Davao

Davao homes are served mainly by Davao Light (DLPC). Davao City is served by Davao Light and Power Company, with high, consistent sun. With grid rates in the Davao Light area among the reasons solar has taken off, a well-sized rooftop system can offset a large share of a typical bill — and net metering lets you export what you do not use for credit, up to a 100 kW system.

What to expect

On Davao roofs

Davao's common house-and-lot homes usually have generous, unshaded roofs, and the city's strong irradiance supports high solar production. As a planning guide, installed cost runs around ₱50,000 per kWp, most homes land between a 3 kWp and 10 kWp system depending on the bill, and a grid-tied system with net metering commonly pays back in about three to five years. Your real figures depend on your roof orientation, shading and daytime usage — which is exactly what the free assessment works out for your specific address.

Questions

Solar in Davao — questions

How much can solar save on a Davao electricity bill?

It depends on your bill and how much power you use during daylight, but many Davao Region homes offset well over half their bill. The free assessment estimates your savings from your actual Davao Light bill, not an average.

Which utility serves Davao?

Davao is served mainly by Davao Light (DLPC). Grid-tied solar with net metering works with it for systems up to 100 kW, crediting your exported energy against what you draw.

Do you have installers in Davao yet?

Coverage is expanding city by city, starting with Metro Manila. If there is no screened installer in Davao when you run your assessment, we tell you honestly — you still get your full bill-backed report.

Which utility handles net metering in Davao?

Davao City is served by Davao Light and Power Company (DLPC). Net metering applies for grid-tied systems up to 100 kW; your installer processes the application with the utility.
