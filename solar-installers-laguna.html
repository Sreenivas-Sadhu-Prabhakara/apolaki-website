Local context

Electricity and solar in Laguna

Most of built-up Laguna — San Pedro, Biñan, Santa Rosa, Cabuyao, Calamba and Los Baños — is served by MERALCO, whose residential rate sat near ₱14 per kWh through 2026; the lakeshore towns of the fourth district, such as Pagsanjan, Paete, Pangil and Siniloan, fall under First Laguna Electric Cooperative (FLECO) instead. Laguna's inland position gives it strong solar resource — a Calamba-area roof produces roughly 1,400 kWh per kWp each year (PVGIS) — so a well-sized rooftop system can offset a large share of a typical bill. Net metering lets you export what you do not use for a credit at the utility's generation rate, up to a 100 kW system.

What to expect

On Laguna roofs

Subdivision houses in Santa Rosa, Nuvali, Biñan and Calamba usually have generous concrete or metal roofs — ideal for a rooftop array — while estates like Laguna Technopark, the Light Industry & Science Park in Cabuyao and Calamba, and Carmelray give the province heavy daytime commercial and industrial load that solar suits especially well. The inland heat that makes Laguna warm also brings high irradiance, though very hot cells run a little less efficiently, so ventilation and panel quality matter; typhoon-season winds call for properly rated mounting. As a planning guide, installed cost typically runs ₱50,000–65,000 per kWp (about ₱50,000 at the entry level), most homes land between a 3 kWp and 10 kWp system depending on the bill, and a grid-tied system with net metering commonly pays back in about three to five years. Your real figures depend on your roof orientation, shading and daytime usage — which is exactly what the free assessment works out for your specific address.

Questions

Solar in Laguna — questions

How much can solar save on a Laguna electricity bill?

It depends on your bill and how much power you use during daylight, but many CALABARZON homes offset well over half their bill. A Calamba-area roof yields roughly 1,400 kWh per kWp a year (PVGIS), and with MERALCO's residential rate near ₱14 per kWh in 2026 the daytime power you use directly is where most savings sit — exports earn a smaller credit at the utility's generation rate, not a full one-to-one swap. The free assessment estimates your savings from your actual MERALCO bill, not an average.

Which utility serves Laguna?

Most of built-up Laguna — San Pedro, Biñan, Santa Rosa, Cabuyao, Calamba and Los Baños — is MERALCO territory, while the lakeshore towns of the fourth district, such as Pagsanjan, Paete and Siniloan, are served by First Laguna Electric Cooperative (FLECO). Either way, grid-tied solar with net metering is applied for through your distribution utility, for systems up to 100 kW.

Does Laguna's heat and typhoon season affect a solar system?

Laguna's warm inland climate — April and May highs near 34°C — means strong sunlight, though very hot cells run slightly less efficiently, so good airflow under the panels and quality hardware matter. Typhoon season, roughly June to November, calls for mounting engineered for local wind loads. A screened installer designs around both, and the assessment flags them for your specific roof.
